## Learning Experiment
When watching this week’s lecture video, I tried different methods of retaining information. My experiment results are below:

Hypothesis: My current method of writing notes while watching the video is the best method to retain information.

Experiment: Split the lecture video into four (somewhat even) parts. Watch these different parts of the lecture video using four methods (later in the day (about 3 or 4 hours), try to recall some of the information):
1.	Watch video only
2.	Watch video plus active recall
3.	Watch video plus teach it out loud
4.	Watch video plus take notes

Result: Watching the video plus active recall was better. I think this is because the type of notes this subject requires is not the same as other subjects in the IT course. It is more to do with how to utilise certain features of applications, rather than just theoretical information to memorise. Furthermore, only watching the video was the worst way to retain information, as I found myself not paying attention very much. Moreover, my usual method of simply writing the lecture slide notes was okay, but I could not recall much later in the day. Lastly, the teaching out loud method was pretty good, however it could be combined with either note taking or active recall (or both) to be more effective.

The steps to doing this experiment are below:

Hypothesis: Identify the method of retaining information you find best here. This could be one you usually use, or one you think would be better.

Experiment: Split the lecture video into four (somewhat even) parts. Watch these different parts of the lecture video using four methods (later in the day (about 3 or 4 hours), try to recall some of the information):
1.	Watch video only
2.	Watch video plus active recall
3.	Watch video plus teach it out loud
4.	Watch video plus take notes

Result: Note down how well you could remember each part of the lecture video. Whichever part you can remember the most from, the method used for that part would be the best one.
